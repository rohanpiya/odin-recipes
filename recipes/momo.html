Momo

Description

Momo is a popular dumpling originally from the Himalayan region, especially Nepal and Tibet. It consists of thin dough wrappers filled with seasoned meat or vegetables, then steamed until tender. Momos are commonly served with a spicy tomato-based dipping sauce.

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup water (approximately)
  • ½ tsp salt

For the Filling

  • 1 lb (450 g) ground chicken, buffalo, pork, or vegetables
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • 2 tbsp chopped cilantro
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Mix the flour, salt, and water to form a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. In a bowl, combine all filling ingredients and mix well.
  3. Divide the dough into small pieces and roll each piece into a thin circular wrapper.
  4. Place about 1 tablespoon of filling in the center of each wrapper.
  5. Fold and pleat the edges to seal the momo completely.
  6. Lightly oil a steamer tray and arrange the momos so they do not touch each other.
  7. Steam for 10–15 minutes, or until the dough becomes slightly translucent and the filling is fully cooked.
  8. Serve hot with momo achar or your preferred dipping sauce.
